# Confidence Scoring

## Definition

Confidence scoring represents the strength of alignment between an input and the defined knowledge.



## Purpose

It provides a measurable indication of how strongly a decision is supported by the evaluated conditions.



## Behavior

- Generated during evaluation
- Reflects consistency with defined knowledge
- Remains stable for identical inputs



## Determinism

- Same input → same confidence
- No randomness or statistical variation



## Interpretation

Confidence indicates:

- Degree of alignment
- Relative strength of the selected outcome

It does not represent probability or prediction.



## Usage

Confidence scoring supports:

- Decision evaluation
- Ranking of outcomes
- Operational insight



## Transparency

Confidence values are derived from deterministic evaluation and are consistent across executions.



## Summary

Confidence scoring reflects how strongly an outcome aligns with defined knowledge.

> It measures alignment, not uncertainty.
